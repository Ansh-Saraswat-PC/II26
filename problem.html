The Crisis.

India’s cities report a single citywide AQI average. This statistical flattening masks dangerous, ward-level pollution spikes, leaving vulnerable populations completely unprotected.

Critical Impact

54,000+

Premature deaths annually in Delhi.

A resident living 400 metres from a construction site, landfill, or biomass-burning zone receives the exact same advisory as a resident in a green belt across the city. The human cost of this generalized data is catastrophic.

Spatial Masking

High-AQI micro-zones are averaged away by current state dashboards. A ward recording AQI 380 looks mathematically identical to a ward recording AQI 120 in city-level aggregations.

Source Ambiguity

Existing monitoring stations report what pollution levels are, not why they are elevated. Without accurate source attribution, municipal administrators cannot select the correct localized intervention.

Reactive Governance

Current public health advisories are issued hours after a spike has occurred, when the physiological damage is already being done. There is no mechanism for forward-looking, proactive alerts.
